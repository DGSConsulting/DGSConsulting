How One Auto Shop Recovered 120 Hours Per Month (Without Hiring Anyone)

A 6-month transformation that turned hidden inefficiencies into $23,400 in annual savings

Share on LinkedIn Email This Download PDF

The Breaking Point

When James and I first connected, his mid-sized auto repair shop in Eastern Canada was working his fourth consecutive 60-hour week. His multi-bay operation with seven skilled technicians had a stellar reputation, loyal customers, and a backlog of work that stretched two weeks out.

By every visible measure, the business was thriving.

But James was drowning.

"We’re booked solid," he said, "and still, I can’t catch up. I spend more time fixing my own paperwork than the cars."

The Diagnosis

The shop’s management systems were fractured. QuickBooks, Shopmonkey, and a handful of spreadsheets weren’t talking to each other. Important details fell through the cracks:

  • Labor hours weren’t tracked properly against jobs
  • Invoices were delayed, causing cash flow headaches
  • Technicians were spending hours on redundant paperwork
  • Shop manager had zero insight into monthly profitability per bay

Hours were being lost every day, and no one could see it.

The Solution

We integrated the shop's core systems and simplified workflows:

  • Connected Shopmonkey and QuickBooks for automatic invoice creation
  • Created a unified dashboard for labor tracking and profitability per bay
  • Standardized SOPs for incoming repairs, technician updates, and client notifications
  • Trained staff on new system workflows, saving 10–15 minutes per job

The goal: make it impossible to "drop the ball" on any repair and eliminate hidden inefficiencies.
