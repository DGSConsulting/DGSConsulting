How One Auto Shop Found $20K Hiding in Plain Sight

Home | All Case Studies

When I first met Mike, owner of a busy auto shop in Ontario, he was exhausted. The shop was thriving — cars lined up outside, phones ringing off the hook — but behind the scenes, chaos ruled. His team of seven technicians were spending almost as much time updating paperwork as they were fixing vehicles.

“We’re not a tech company,” Mike said. “I just want my guys fixing cars, not playing secretary.”

The Hidden Money Drain

Their techs were finishing jobs, then spending 15–20 minutes updating three disconnected systems:

  • A scheduling board that was constantly out of date
  • A separate inventory system that required manual stock entry
  • An invoicing tool that didn’t sync with either system

Multiply that across 8–10 jobs daily, and the shop was losing over 120 hours per month — the equivalent of a full-time salary wasted on data entry instead of revenue-generating work.

Customers were waiting longer for repairs, staff morale was sinking, and Mike was burning out trying to hold everything together.

The Simple Fix That Changed Everything

Instead of overhauling everything overnight, we tackled the problem in three simple steps:

  • Map the Workflow: We shadowed the team for two days, documenting every step from check-in to invoice.
  • Choose the Right System: We selected software that fit their existing habits — no massive learning curve required.
  • Train Without Pain: A two-hour training session was all it took for the team to get comfortable and excited.

The result? Jobs now close with a couple of clicks. Inventory updates automatically. Invoices and payments sync in real time.

Key Lessons for Small Businesses

This project reinforced three lessons I see again and again in small businesses:

  • Technology only works if it fits the way people work.
  • Small, focused changes beat big, disruptive overhauls.
  • The hidden costs of inefficiency are almost always bigger than expected.
