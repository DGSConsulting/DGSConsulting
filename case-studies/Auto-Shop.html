How One Auto Shop Found $20K Hiding in Plain Sight

A real-world case study in operational efficiency and system integration

When I first met Mike, owner of a busy auto shop in Ottawa, he was exhausted. The shop was bustling — cars lined up outside, calls pouring in — yet behind the scenes, chaos reigned.

His team of seven technicians spent nearly as much time filling out paperwork and updating systems as they did fixing vehicles. “We’re not a tech company,” Mike said. “I just want my guys fixing cars, not playing secretary.”

The Hidden Money Drain

The techs were finishing jobs, then spending 15–20 minutes updating three disconnected systems:

  • A scheduling board that was always out of sync
  • An inventory system requiring manual updates
  • An invoicing tool that didn’t interface with either

With 8–10 jobs per day, that meant over 120 hours per month were eaten up by redundant data entry — essentially a full-time salary squandered on administrative tasks.

Delays piled up, morale dipped, and Mike felt like he was always one step behind.

The Simple Fix That Changed Everything

We approached the problem in three clear phases — minimizing disruption while gaining maximum impact:

  • Workflow Mapping: Shadowed the team for two days to document every step from check-in to invoice.
  • Smart Tool Selection: Chose a system that mirrored their real-world workflow, not forced them into new habits.
  • Painless Training: Delivered a two-hour hands-on session that got immediate buy-in — techs saw the benefit instantly.

The result: jobs close with a few clicks, inventory updates automatically, and invoices and payments sync seamlessly.
