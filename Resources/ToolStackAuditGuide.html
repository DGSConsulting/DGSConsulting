Tool Stack Audit Guide

A practical, SMB-focused guide to cut costs, reduce complexity, and build a tool stack that actually helps your business.

Clarity over complexity.

I help small business owners and consultants reclaim their time and profit by simplifying the systems they already use, not adding more tools. This guide shows you exactly how to start cleaning up your tech stack with calm confidence and zero overwhelm.

Why this matters for small businesses

Most small businesses don’t plan to build a complicated tech stack it happens over time. A trial becomes a subscription. Someone adds a tool that only one person uses. Before long, you have more logins, more bills, and more friction.

This guide gives you a clear, step-by-step process to clean up your tools, stop wasting money, and speed up day-to-day work.

1. Start with a complete list

Write down every tool: subscriptions, extensions, AI add-ons, payment tools, project apps, storage everything with a cost or a login.

For each tool, capture:

  • What it does
  • How often you use it
  • How much it costs
  • When it renews
  • Who uses it and why
  • Whether another tool already does the same job

Tip: a simple spreadsheet is enough. If you want a fast way to turn process decisions into repeatable SOPs, use the SOP Designer to capture roles, owners, and how tools connect to your processes.

2. Group tools by purpose

Sort tools into plain categories so overlap becomes obvious:

  • Operations project management, docs, tasks
  • Sales & Marketing CRM, email, social
  • Finance & Admin invoicing, payroll, contracts
  • Automation workflow/connectors
  • Productivity storage, calendar, messaging

3. Evaluate what’s helping vs. hurting

For each tool ask:

  • Do we use this regularly?
  • Does it make work easier or add steps?
  • Is it duplicating something else?
  • Is the price justified?
  • Does it integrate well with the rest of the stack?

4. Find time and cost drains

Many issues hide inside workflows not the tools themselves. Use the Time Drain Detector to identify the tasks and tools that consume the most time so your decisions are based on evidence, not guesswork.

5. Score each tool (1–5)

Rate every tool in these areas: Impact, Usage, Ease, Cost Value, Fit. Low-scoring tools are clear candidates for removal.

6. Decide: Keep, Replace, Consolidate, Cancel

Sort tools into one of four outcomes. Consolidation replacing several tools with one that does multiple jobs is where SMBs save the most.

7. Build your lean stack

A good SMB stack is simple, integrated, and easy to train on. Example:

  • Operations Notion or ClickUp
  • Communication Slack or Google Chat
  • CRM HubSpot Free
  • Email MailerLite or ConvertKit
  • Automation Make
  • Finance Wave or QuickBooks + Stripe
  • Creative Canva
  • AI ChatGPT

8. Document everything

Record what you use, why you use it, who owns it, renewal dates, and integrations. The SOP Designer makes this step fast: turn decisions into SOPs and handoffs so tool ownership survives hires and role changes.

9. Re-audit regularly

Review tools every 6–12 months or whenever you hire, change processes, or add new services. Regular checks prevent sprawl from coming back.

Quick wins (start here)

  • Cancel unused premium plans
  • Consolidate overlapping apps
  • Create one docs source of truth (Notion)
  • Automate manual transfers with Make
  • Use the Time Drain Detector to prioritize fixes
