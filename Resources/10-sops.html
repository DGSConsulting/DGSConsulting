If your business relies on memory, it’s not a system — it’s a liability. When key people leave, so does your knowledge. SOPs solve that. They make your processes transferable, consistent, and scalable — the difference between “organized chaos” and actual structure.

1. Client Onboarding

Purpose: Set the tone for every client relationship.

  • Signed contract received
  • Kickoff call scheduled
  • Shared workspace / CRM access created
  • Welcome email sent with expectations

2. Lead Management

Purpose: Define how leads move from contact to conversion.

  • Log leads in CRM
  • Set automated 48-hour follow-up
  • Track stage updates weekly

3. Invoicing & Payments

Purpose: Keep cash flow consistent and predictable.

  • Document who generates invoices
  • Set schedule (e.g., every Monday for completed work)
  • Define payment methods and overdue follow-ups

4. Project Kickoff

Purpose: Start every project with clarity and aligned expectations.

  • Confirm scope and objectives
  • Assign roles and responsibilities
  • Define success metrics
  • Schedule first milestone review

5. Quality Control

Purpose: Ensure what you deliver matches your promises.

  • Internal review by at least two team members
  • Checklists for project deliverables
  • Document approval before client delivery

6. Customer Support

Purpose: Handle issues consistently and efficiently.

  • Tier 1: common inquiries (resolve within 1 business day)
  • Tier 2: escalations to management
  • Tier 3: urgent / legal issues
